<p><strong>REPETITION STATEMENTS</strong></p>
<p>&nbsp; Java provides three repetition statements (also called looping statements) that enable programs to perform statements repeatedly as long as a condition (called the loop-continuation condition) remains true. <br /> <br /> <strong><u>These statements are :<br /> </u><br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; 1)&nbsp; for statement<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; 2)&nbsp; While statement<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; 3)&nbsp; do...while statement</strong><br /> <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; The while and for statements perform the action (or group of actions) in their bodies zero or more times; if the loop-continuation condition is initially false, the action (or group of actions) will not execute.<br /> <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; The do...while statement performs the action (or group of actions) in its body one or more times<br /> <br /> <strong>FOR STATEMENT<br /> </strong><br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; A for statement is an iteration statement (also called a for-loop statement), which is used to loop through a statement for a number of times based on some conditions.<br /> <br /> <strong><u>The general form of a for-loop statement is :<br /> </u><br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; for (initialization; condition-expression; expression-list)<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Statement<br /> </strong><br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; For example, the following for-loop statement will print all integers between 1 and 10, inclusive:<br /> <br /> <strong>&nbsp;&nbsp; for(int num = 1; num &lt;= 10; num++)<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; System.out.println(num); <br /> </strong><br /> <strong>WHILE STATEMENT<br /> </strong><br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; A while statement is another iteration (or, loop) statement, which is used to execute a statement repeatedly as long as a condition is true.<br /> <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; A while statement is also known as a while-loop statement.<br /> <br /> <strong><u>The general form of a while-loop statement is :<br /> </u></strong><br /> <strong>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Initialization<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; while (condition-expression) {<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Statement<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Expression-list<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; } <br /> </strong><br /> For example, You can print all integers between 1 and 10 using a while-loop as shown:<br /> <strong><br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; int i = 1;<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; while (i &lt;= 10) {<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; System.out.println(i);<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; i++;<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; } <br /> </strong><br /> <strong>DO...WHILE STATEMENT<br /> </strong><br /> &nbsp;&nbsp; The do-while statement is another loop statement. It is similar to the while-loop statement with one difference. The statement associated with a while loop statement may not be executed even once if the condition-expression evaluates to false for the first time.<br /> <br /> However, the statement associated with a do-while statement is executed at least once. <br /> <br /> <strong><u>The general form of a do-while statement is:<br /> </u></strong><br /> <br /> <strong>&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; do<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; Statement<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; while (condition-expression);</strong> <br /> <br /> For example, A do-while loop can compute the sum of integers between 1 and 10 as shown:<br /> <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>int i = 1;<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; int sum = 0;<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; do {<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; sum = sum + i; <br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; i++;<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; }<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; while (i &lt;= 10);<br /> &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; System.out.println("Sum = " + sum); <br /> </strong><br /> <br /> <strong>************END OF LECTURE FIVE*************</strong></p>
